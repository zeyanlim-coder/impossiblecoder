GTM · 01
Ideal Customer Profile
How we identify and score restaurants before any outreach begins. Everything downstream depends on getting this right.
Brand Power Signals
Google Reviews
Volume and rating — proxy for brand establishment, credibility, and cult following.
Instagram Crawl
Number of posts shared about the brand — proxy for virality and trendiness.
Both signals are crawled by the RevOps team and used to compute the brand power score.
Restaurant Archetype
Scored by
Number of outlets + average spend per customer.
Sweet spot
Fast casual · Casual · Premium casual
High turnover, sufficient transaction count, enough data to generate real value for the merchant.
Excluded — low end
QSR — average spend too low to drive meaningful GMV.
Excluded — high end
Fine dining — spend high but transaction volume too low for data products to work.
Score Output
High score pool
Strong brand power + right archetype. Priority for direct sales outreach sprint.
Low score pool
Weaker signals. Reached via AI outreach and paid ads.
Product suitability
Score also determines which Oddle products fit — e.g. large-seating popular restaurants score high for Reserve. Feeds into the prospecting guide during pitch.
Lead Score — Current Model (0–100)
What it is
The current potential score. Computed by the RevOps pipeline and pushed to Attio automatically.
Score components
score_reviews (Google rating + review count) + score_ig_followers (Instagram size) + score_ig_recency (posting activity)
Pipeline
Apify GMB crawl → SerpAPI Instagram search → Apify Instagram crawl → weighted scoring → Attio CRM push. Runs on GCP, triggered from a single command.
Used for
ICP prioritisation (high score pool vs low score pool), prospecting guide generation, and the 'potential' axis in the CS 2×2 tiering matrix.
Evolving → Total Potential Value Model
Direction
The lead_score will evolve into a sum of per-product potential values — a more precise ceiling for each merchant.
Total Potential Value = PV(Oddle Shop) + PV(Oddle Reserve) + PV(Oddle Enrolment)
Why it's better
Two restaurants with the same lead_score can have very different product fit profiles. TPV captures this — e.g. a large-seating restaurant scores high on Reserve PV even if its Instagram is quiet.
Per-product PV formula
Methodology pending — to be defined based on restaurant profile signals (outlet count, seating, spend per head, transaction volume, etc.) per product.
⚠ Once defined, this replaces lead_score as the potential axis in the 2×2 tiering matrix.
GTM · 02
TAM Crawl & Contact Enrichment
RevOps builds and enriches the full restaurant universe before outreach begins. Data quality here determines everything downstream.
TAM Crawl
What
RevOps team crawls the internet to build a complete universe of all restaurants in the market.
Scored on
Brand power (Google + Instagram), archetype (outlet count + spend per head), current tech stack.
Tech stack mapped
Whitelabel delivery / takeaway channels, marketplace presence, existing reservation solution type.
Tech stack feeds directly into the sales rep's prospecting guide — pain points pre-identified before the first call.
Contact Enrichment
Email
Crawled from public sources and enriched per restaurant.
Phone — outlet
Store outlet number. Baseline contact for all restaurants.
Phone — owner
Owner or mobile number where findable. Significantly increases conversion when the decision maker is reached directly.
Pipeline Architecture (5 Steps)
Step 1 — IG Enrichment + Lead Scoring
SerpAPI finds Instagram handle → Apify crawls followers + last 5 posts → scoring computed (score_reviews, score_ig_followers, score_ig_recency → lead_score 0–100). Runs in parallel with Step 2.
Step 2 — Tech Platform Detection
SerpAPI searches delivery/reservation pages → fuzzy match + Claude Haiku validates → HTML scrape extracts delivery_partners and reservation_partner. Runs in parallel with Step 1.
Step 3 — Merchant Matching
Fuzzy + GPT-4o mini matches brand against existing merchants in BigQuery. Outputs is_existing_merchant and brand_id.
Step 4 — Attio CRM Push
Fetches or creates Attio record by place_id. Updates if exists, creates if new. Existing merchants flagged automatically.
Step 5 — Contact Enrichment
Swan tool runs independently. One brand → many contacts. Writes to separate contacts table. No dependency on CRM push.
Infrastructure
GCP Cloud Run + Scheduler. Apify, SerpAPI, Claude Haiku, GPT-4o mini, BigQuery, Attio, Swan. Pipeline is safe to rerun at any time — each step writes back to places_clean immediately.
LLM Match Threshold
> 85% confidence
Accept automatically.
60–85% confidence
LLM verification step triggered.
< 60% confidence
Rejected.
Voice Agent — Owner Number Extraction
What
AI voice agent calls all restaurants and runs scripted sequences to extract the owner's direct number.
Scripts
Multiple variants tested and optimized for compliance and response rate.
Why it matters
Getting to the decision maker directly is the highest-leverage enrichment step in the whole GTM.

GTM · 06
Follow-Up Agent
Keeps warm deals moving without the sales rep having to remember. Rep is only pulled in when a human touch is needed.
The Problem It Solves
Root cause
Reps carry a rolling 90-day pipeline. As pitch volume grows, follow-ups on in-negotiation deals get dropped — not by neglect, but by crowding.
Why deals go quiet
Owners drag their feet, got busy and forgot, feel no urgency, or are half-convinced and need one more push.
Key insight
If it's still in negotiation, it is not a no. The agent's job is to remove friction and create urgency.

CS · 08
LIR Framework
Leading Indicator of Retention. The single north star metric for the entire CS motion.
Core Definition
LIR achieved when
Monthly Product Value ≥ $215
Within the first 30 days of going live.
Formula
(Shop GMV × $0.50) + (Reserve Bookings × $2) + (Enrolments × $1)
Cohort target
60% of merchants hit LIR within 30 days of going live.
Why it matters
LIR is the leading indicator — it tells us whether a merchant will renew before we see the lagging indicator of actual retention.
Per-Product Standalone Thresholds
Oddle Shop
$500 GMV/month
$1 GMV = $0.50 value → $250 value
Oddle Reserve
100 bookings/month
$2 per booking → $200 value
Oddle Enrolment
200 enrolments/month
$1 per enrolment → $200 value
Composite LIR (Multi-Product)
The insight
A merchant struggling to hit LIR on a single product may be one additional product away from strong retention. Cross-selling is a retention play, not just a revenue play.
Example
Shop $300 GMV ($150) + Reserve 50 bookings ($100) + Enrolment 100 ($100) = $350 total — exceeds $215 threshold even though none hit standalone LIR.
LIR Cohort Dashboard
Format
BigQuery → automated. Each row = cohort of merchants that went live in a given month. Each column = % achieving LIR by month 1, 2, 3 etc.
Status
Being built right now.
Priority
This should be the first slide in every board deck — ahead of revenue. It is the leading indicator of business health.

Tier Definitions — Post 45-Day Review
First 45 days — all merchants
CSM owns this window without exception. Onboarding, activation, LIR drive. No tiering yet.
Tier 1 — KAM (top 20%)
Dedicated KAM. Quarterly business reviews, strategic growth planning, priority support.
Tier 2 — PV-Driven Scaled Motion
PV-targeted email and WhatsApp content for all non-KAM merchants. Content segmented by each merchant's highest unrealised product PV — not generic. When PV signal for a specific product crosses a threshold, escalates to the Expansion Role for human follow-up.
No distinction between middle 50% and bottom 30% — the PV engine handles segmentation automatically.
⚠ New Role — Product Expansion (Seat to Design)
What they own
Product adoption and expansion for all non-KAM merchants where PV threshold is crossed. Not onboarding, not strategic account management.
Trigger
Merchant's PV for a second product crosses a defined threshold. System escalates automatically.
Channel
Email and WhatsApp primarily. Human engagement reserved for high-PV escalations.
Why not CSM
CSM will have their plate full with new merchant onboarding as acquisition scales. This keeps CS focused on activation and LIR.
Status
Seat designed. Not yet filled. PV-driven scaled motion runs in the interim.
Hidden Gems — Recovery Playbook
Root cause 1
Setup issue — product not configured optimally (menu incomplete, booking widget not on Google).
Root cause 2
Awareness issue — merchant not promoting the product to their customers.
Root cause 3
Execution issue — staff not trained to support the product.
Root cause 4
Expectation issue — merchant expected a different outcome from sales promises.
Action
CSM diagnostic call → 30-day recovery plan. If recovered: move up tier. If not: flag pre-sign score to RevOps for model adjustment.

CS · 14
RevOps Feedback Loop
The scoring model is only as good as its ability to predict outcomes. This loop is what makes it improve over time.
What Feeds Back to RevOps
LIR by lead score band
Are high-scored leads actually achieving LIR? If not, the scoring model is wrong.
Hidden Gems
High potential, low performance. The model overweighted certain signals — which ones?
Overperformers
Low potential, high performance. What did the scoring model miss?
Time to LIR by segment
Which segments achieve LIR fastest? This should inform sales targeting.
Churn reasons
Why are merchants leaving? Does this correlate with any pre-sign signals?
Feedback Cadence
Monthly
Automated dashboard — LIR achievement by lead score band, segment, and product. Shared with RevOps automatically.
Quarterly
CS + RevOps alignment meeting. Review 2×2 matrix, identify scoring model improvements, agree on targeting adjustments.
Ad hoc
CSMs flag individual cases where scoring was significantly off — via a structured form in Attio.
The Virtuous Cycle
Better scoring → Better targeting → Higher-quality sign-ups
→ Higher LIR achievement → Better retention → More data → Better scoring
Long-term goal
Pre-sign potential score converges with actual current ability — sales is consistently signing merchants who succeed.
